Welcome to my silly little site.

Welcome to my silly little site, it’s a mess and I have a lot of interests. I’m currently pursuing a gender-neutral honorific as a graduate student in Dr. Gretchen Daily’s Lab, housed in Stanford’s Biology Department, which lies on the unceded lands of the Muwekma Ohlone, who continually fight for their right to be recognized federally and preserve as much of their language and culture as possible. If you have the means to do so, please donate to their gofundme to help with their educational programs, especially those that work, live, or benefit from these lands.

Right now I’d consider myself an urban ecologist, seeing as I study mostly urban systems including urban wildlife communities, restorative community greenspaces, and harmful legacies of historical and contemporary urban policies.

I’ve always enjoyed incorporating art into science so you’ll see some comics, doodles, and other visualizations of sorts sprinkled throughout my work. Sometimes I do science-science, like working with large datasets and struggling to navigate through poison oak and blackberry thorns in the field. I love to code and create interactive maps, tinkering around the intersection of traditional and digital art has been exciting. I also am not very good at coming up with my own questions but I am good at squandering resources– which is why I tend to focus on local community and justice-based research (CBR). [Resources] if you want to learn more about this framework. The world sucks and so do people in power including scientists from elite institutions, so I try to do less of that and provide myself as a tool for folks on the ground who already have been doing the work and hold local knowledge.
